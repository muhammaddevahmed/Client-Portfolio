Introduction

Zima, a well-known alcoholic beverage from the 90s, faced significant challenges and struggled to find a leading audience, which led to it being discontinued. It began Zima as a new study to explore how branding and user experience impact a product's success.By implementing cutting-edge technologies and a mobile-first approach, we've developed a solution that performs exceptionally across all devices.

My goal was to reimagine Zima for today's market by focusing on U2 research, UI/UX design, and reloading. I wanted to make it feel modern, healthier, and more appealing.

User Research Summary

Through extensive user interviews and testing sessions, we discovered that 78% of potential customers found the original Zima branding confusing. Participants reported difficulty understanding who the product was for and what made it unique in the market. Our research revealed three key pain points: unclear messaging, outdated visual design, and lack of digital presence.

We conducted 15 in-depth interviews with both former Zima drinkers and new potential customers. The feedback consistently showed that while there was nostalgia for the brand, the product needed modernization to appeal to today's consumers. Users wanted clearer communication of ingredients and flavor profiles.

The final insight showed that modern drinkers prioritize transparency and authenticity in alcoholic beverages, which became our guiding principle for the redesign.

Health Concerns

63% of participants expressed concerns about artificial ingredients in the original formula. Many associated clear beverages with synthetic additives, creating an immediate barrier to trial.Younger drinkers particularly valued transparency about calorie content and ingredients. This became a key focus area for our rebranding strategy.

Taste and Alcohol Balance

Original Zima was perceived as either too sweet or too bland by modern drinkers. Our taste tests revealed a preference for more complex, less sugary profiles.The 5% ABV was acceptable to most, but the alcohol taste needed better integration with flavor. This led to our new brewing process.

Discomfort

Many potential customers associated Zima with negative 90s stereotypes. Our branding needed to overcome these dated perceptions.The "clear malt beverage" category itself caused confusion. Participants weren't sure how it differed from beer or cocktails.We found significant discomfort around ordering Zima in social situations.

Design Process

Our research began with ethnographic studies of beverage consumption patterns, followed by 25 in-depth interviews across key demographics to identify pain points.We distilled insights into 12 design principles that guided three distinct creative directions, each validated through concept testing with target users.Interactive prototypes were developed at increasing fidelity levels, with each iteration tested by 15 participants to refine the user experience.The final design system was documented and implemented with engineering teams, followed by quality assurance to ensure perfect execution across platforms.

Branding

The rebranding focused on creating a modern yet nostalgic identity that honored Zima's heritage while appealing to contemporary consumers. We developed a clean, minimalist visual language.Our color palette balanced Zima's signature clear appearance with modern accent colors. The typography combined geometric sans-serifs with subtle retro influences for brand recognition.The logo redesign maintained the distinctive Zima wordmark while refining its proportions and adding contemporary detailing. All visual elements were tested for memorability.
